// Defining the Threat
What Is
Cyber Harassment?

Cyber harassment is the use of digital technology to intimidate, threaten, humiliate, or harm another person. It occurs across social media, messaging apps, emails, and online platforms — affecting millions of people worldwide every day.

👁
CYBER STALKING
Repeatedly monitoring someone's online activity, tracking their location, or sending persistent unwanted messages. Victims feel watched, unsafe, and violated — even in their own homes.
🛡 Protect Yourself

Review your privacy settings. Never share real-time location publicly. Block and report any stalker accounts immediately.

💬
ONLINE BULLYING
Sending hateful messages, public humiliation, spreading rumors, or deliberately excluding someone. 1 in 3 young people experience cyberbullying — leading to severe emotional and mental harm.
🛡 Protect Yourself

Do not engage with bullies. Save all evidence. Report to platform administrators and trusted adults or authorities.

📢
HATE SPEECH
Online content that attacks individuals based on race, religion, gender, or identity. Hate speech incites violence, spreads fear, and creates toxic digital environments for entire communities.
🛡 Protect Yourself

Report hateful content immediately. Mute or block offenders. Support affected communities. Never amplify hate by sharing it.

📍
DOXING
Publicly exposing someone's private information — address, phone number, workplace — without consent. Doxing can lead to real-world danger, harassment campaigns, and physical threats.
🛡 Protect Yourself

Limit personal info shared online. Use private email addresses. Regularly audit what information is publicly available about you.

🎭
IMPERSONATION
Creating fake profiles or accounts to deceive others, damage reputations, or manipulate situations. Impersonators can destroy relationships, careers, and mental well-being of victims.
🛡 Protect Yourself

Enable two-factor authentication. Report fake accounts to platforms. Alert your contacts if you discover a fake profile of you.

📰
DISINFORMATION
Deliberately spreading false information to damage reputation or manipulate public opinion. False narratives spread 6× faster than truth online — causing real psychological and social damage.
🛡 Protect Yourself

Verify before sharing. Use trusted news sources. Report false content. Critical thinking is your strongest defense.

// Defense Strategies
How To Protect Yourself
01

🔒 Lock Your Privacy

Set all accounts to private. Control who can see your posts, location, and personal information.

02

📸 Document Everything

Screenshot all harassment. Keep records with dates and times before blocking the offender.

03

🚨 Report & Block

Use platform report tools. Contact cybercrime authorities. Don't suffer in silence — report it.

04

🤝 Seek Support

Talk to trusted family, friends, or professionals. You are not alone — help is always available.
