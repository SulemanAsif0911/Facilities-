After Reporting

Case Tracker and Status Updates

Use this section to explain the normal path after filing with an official portal: evidence, complaint submission, acknowledgement, review, follow-up, and safety planning.

Track a local draft or official number

Enter a local draft ID from this page or an official complaint number.
  1. Save evidence before blocking or deleting messages.
  2. Submit the complaint through the official NCCIA/FIA portal or office.
  3. Keep the acknowledgement number and any email or SMS replies.
  4. Follow up with the official office or helpline if there is no response.
  5. Update passwords, privacy settings, and trusted contacts.

Content Library

Harassment Type Library

Short, practical explainers for the incidents Pakistani students and families most often need to understand quickly.

Cyber Stalking

Repeated monitoring, unwanted messages, threats, or tracking across accounts.

  • Save usernames, links, timestamps, and screenshots.
  • Record repeated contact patterns before blocking.
  • PECA may treat repeated online surveillance or unwanted contact as cyber stalking.

Doxing

Sharing private phone numbers, addresses, photos, or family details to intimidate someone.

  • Capture the public post, comments, and share links.
  • Ask trusted people not to engage with the post.
  • Report to the platform and official channels if safety is at risk.

Sextortion

Threatening to share private or intimate content to force money, silence, or more content.

  • Do not pay or send more material.
  • Save threats, payment demands, accounts, and phone numbers.
  • Use official complaint channels and contact a trusted person immediately.

Impersonation

Fake accounts using someone’s name, photos, school, workplace, or identity details.

  • Copy the profile URL and screenshot the profile and messages.
  • Report the fake account inside the platform.
  • Identity misuse can be relevant under PECA provisions on identity information.

Hate Speech

Targeted abuse based on religion, gender, ethnicity, disability, or identity.

  • Save the original post and surrounding context.
  • Report content that incites harm or coordinated abuse.
  • Escalate urgent threats to police or official cybercrime channels.

Disinformation

False claims, edited media, or smear posts designed to damage reputation.

  • Save the original post before it is deleted or edited.
  • Collect proof showing the claim is false.
  • Avoid public arguments that may spread the content further.

Know Your Rights

Legal Rights and Urdu Support

This is awareness information, not legal advice. Cyber laws can be amended, so users should verify details with official sources, lawyers, or rights organizations before relying on penalties.

PECA 2016 in Simple Words

Pakistan’s Prevention of Electronic Crimes Act covers several electronic offences, including misuse of identity information, privacy/modesty harms, cyber stalking, spoofing, and other computer-related offences.

  • Relevant sections can depend on facts and evidence.
  • Punishments may include fines and imprisonment.
  • Serious threats, intimate-image abuse, minors, or physical danger need urgent support.

Official References

Link users to official material instead of copying long legal text into the page.

  • PCSW PECA overview
  • Pakistan Code PECA PDF
  • NCCIA complaint portal
If someone is in immediate physical danger, they should contact local emergency services or a trusted nearby adult/person first. A website guide should support safety decisions, not delay urgent help.

Urdu Language Support

Quick Safety Guide

Save screenshots, links, usernames, dates, and phone numbers. Do not delete chats before documenting them. Change passwords, enable two factor authentication, and report through official channels when safe.
